# Textures: Evals and Corpus as Source of Truth

Date: 2026-05-11

## Context

Worldsmith treats `specs/` as the source of truth for every domain: the
Extractor distills `reference/` into `knowledge/`, the Architect formalizes
knowledge into specs, and the Coder generates code from specs. This works
because the underlying material -- game mechanics, geometry, tuning numbers --
can be written down as formal constraints with citations and reproduced by a
deterministic generator.

Textures do not fit that mold. Aesthetic identity is not naturally describable
as a finite set of numeric constraints. The tools that would produce them
(diffusion-style image synthesis and vision-language critique) are not
idempotent: running the same prompt twice yields different bytes, and a
vision-LLM's verdict on the same image varies between runs. A pipeline that
regenerated textures from specs on every PR, the way `pr.yml` regenerates
Rust, would churn the visual identity of the game with every merge.

We need a parallel operational pipeline for the texture domain that preserves
identity across iterations, frames the work as drift-detection rather than
art-generation, and stays clearly separated from the spec-driven core.

## Decision

In the texture domain, source of truth is:

- `reference/textures/{good,bad}/` -- an operator-curated corpus of acceptable
  identity (`good/`) and drift examples (`bad/`). Image binaries are
  gitignored; only READMEs are committed.
- `evals/textures/*` -- mechanical drift detectors implemented as scripts
  under `tooling/texture_evals/`, with their inputs, outputs, and thresholds
  documented in markdown alongside the corpus.
- `evals/textures/identity.md` -- a constitution describing the identity
  through constraints (Forbidden, Required, Desired feel, Calibration parking
  lot). The constitution is sanitization-gated the same way `knowledge/*.md`
  is.

`specs/` and `ir/` may refer to texture slots by stable ID (so other systems
can reference a slot without describing its pixels), but they never describe
pixel content. For every domain outside textures, `specs/` remains the source
of truth per CLAUDE.md.

## Consequences

- The Coder, Architect, Reconciler, and PostMortem agents do not generate or
  modify textures. Their phases stay scoped to the spec-driven core and to
  the existing knowledge/specs/IR/code chain.
- Accepted textures are committed checkpoints in their own right, not
  artifacts regenerated by `pr.yml`. Because diffusion and vision-LLM
  critique are non-idempotent, regenerating on every PR would destroy
  identity continuity; instead, an accepted texture is treated like an
  accepted ADR -- a record, not a derivation.
- The existing `tooling/run_evals.py` (which builds and `cargo test`s the
  generated game) and the new `tooling/texture_evals/` (which run
  drift-detection on the texture corpus) share the word "evals" but are
  different systems. The `evals/textures/README.md` is responsible for
  flagging the name collision; neither system is renamed in this PR.
- The sanitization gate enforced by `tooling/validate_specs.py` is extended
  to cover `evals/textures/*.md` in addition to `knowledge/*.md`. The same
  forbidden-token rules apply: no proper nouns of any source or reference
  game, no release-year sentinels, no source-code identifiers from a
  reference.
- The reference-emptiness check in `tooling/validate_specs.py` and the
  matching pre-flight in `.github/workflows/pr.yml` are extended to exclude
  the `reference/textures/` subtree from the determination. Texture-corpus
  content is orthogonal to the knowledge-extraction lifecycle, so its
  presence must not block the existing Extractor-must-stop-when-empty
  guarantee.
- Agent prompts in `tooling/agents/*.md` are NOT updated in this PR.
  Migrating those prompts to know about `adr/` and the texture domain is a
  deliberate separate PR, keeping each change small and auditable.
